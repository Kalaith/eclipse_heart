upgraded forms keep the base identity, so the character still feels like herself

I’m also going to make the upgraded abilities work whether they’re a Main/Prime or a transformed support, since you want the whole cast to matter.

Yuki, full ladder
1. Base Form
Yuki, Moonlit Witness

Power: 2

If Main
Whenever your MG side gains Strain during Encounter, all revealed Support MGs gain 1 Bond.

If Support — Sudden Rescue
Reveal during Encounter: Prevent up to 2 Strain your MG side would gain this Encounter. Then Yuki gains 1 Bond.

What she does

Base Yuki says:

pressure on the team creates closeness
reveals matter
protection is rewarded

She is a support-loving protector, not a raw beater.

2. Transformed Form
Yuki, Lantern Oath

Power: 3

Keeps Base text

Ability I — Shared Bravery
Once each Encounter, when a revealed MG you control gains Bond, prevent 1 Strain your MG side would gain from one source this Encounter.

Why this works

This is a good first-stage unlock because it is:

useful
tactical
not explosive
very “Yuki”

It rewards the same play pattern as her base form:

reveal supports
build Bond
convert that devotion into stability

It also works if transformed Yuki is a support who was revealed early and later powered up.

3. Radiant Form
Yuki, Radiant Moon Aegis

Power: 5

Keeps Base text
Keeps Shared Bravery

Ability II — Moonlit Promise
Once during Final Climax, when your MG side would gain Strain from one source, prevent all of it. Then up to 2 revealed MGs you control each gain 1 Bond.

Why this works

This is the proper “save the day” heroine moment:

huge defensive swing
not an instant win button
strengthens the team for the rest of the scene
still feels like Yuki’s identity, just turned all the way up
Yuki’s play pattern
early: let pressure build team Bond
mid: transformed Yuki converts Bond into stabilization
late: Radiant Yuki creates the clutch reversal that keeps the team standing through Final Climax
Noctra, full ladder
1. Base Form
Noctra, Velvet Eclipse

Power: 2

If Prime
Whenever an opposing revealed Support MG gains Bond during Encounter, Noctra gains 1 Dread.

If Support — Curtain Pierce
Reveal during Encounter: Exhaust an opposing revealed Support MG. If you do, the opposing MG side gains 1 Strain.

What she does

Base Noctra says:

if your team rallies around each other, she feeds on it
if your supports reveal, she punishes the timing
she is Yuki’s clean mechanical rival
2. Awakened Form
Noctra, Eclipse Unbound

Power: 3

Keeps Base text

Ability I — Velvet Pressure
Once each Encounter, when an opposing revealed MG gains Bond, choose one:

the opposing MG side gains 1 Strain
Noctra gains 1 Dread
Why this works

This is a strong first-stage villain unlock because it makes every protective, bonding play feel more dangerous.

It does not instantly crush the opponent. It just says:

your teamwork helps you
but it also pushes the episode closer to breaking

Very fitting for Noctra.

3. Catastrophe Form
Noctra, Midnight Catastrophe

Power: 5

Keeps Base text
Keeps Velvet Pressure

Ability II — Eclipse of Trust
Once during Final Climax, choose an opposing revealed MG. It cannot gain Bond for the rest of this Encounter. Then, if it already has 2 or more Bond, the opposing MG side gains 1 Strain.

Why this works

This is a strong villain climax move because it:

attacks the exact engine Yuki wants
turns an established support piece against the team
feels cruel and dramatic
is powerful, but not a one-card checkmate
Noctra’s play pattern
early: punish reveal timing
mid: awakened Noctra turns Bond into pressure or menace
late: Catastrophe Noctra shuts down the emotional engine at the worst possible moment
Why these two work as rivals
Yuki says:

“Pressure makes us protect each other, and protection makes us stronger.”

Noctra says:

“Every act of protection makes you easier to unravel.”

That is a very good rival axis because they are fighting over the same space:

revealed supports
Bond gain
timing of pressure

Not just generic hero vs villain stats.

Quick balance notes
Power curve

These numbers feel right for your current prototype:

Base: 2
First upgrade: 3
Final upgrade: 5

I would keep that for most mains/primes.

Supports might sometimes start at 1, but Yuki and Noctra being 2 at base feels good because they are marquee characters.

Why Yuki’s first upgrade is defensive

That helps stop early transforms from becoming instant blowouts.
Transformed Yuki helps you survive and stabilize.
Radiant Yuki is where the huge swing happens.

Why Noctra’s first upgrade is pressure, not lockdown

Awakened Noctra increases danger, but does not fully shut the opponent out.
Catastrophe Noctra is where the cruel denial enters.

That makes the staircase feel fair.

Extra polish option

If you want the upgraded forms to be easier to read on cards, you can keyword their unlocked abilities.

Example:

Yuki
Shared Bravery
Moonlit Promise
Noctra
Velvet Pressure
Eclipse of Trust

Then later cards can reference those styles without printing giant walls of text.

My recommendation

I’d use these two as the exact pattern for the remaining 8 characters:

Base identity
first upgrade = tactical ability
final upgrade = climax ability
keep the character’s core loop intact across all forms
